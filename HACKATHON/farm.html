FARM MANAGEMENT


  1. CROP SELECTION ADVICE
    Different crops require different soils, nutrients, and amounts of water. The growing season and the climate where the plant is cultivated also affect how much water it needs. The key to effective crop farming is proper crop selection. Several crucial criteria for crop selection include;
    • The specific location of farm
    • Land availbility
    • Soil
    • Climate
    • Initial Financial investement and return
    • Market demand
    • Water quality and availbility
    • Personal Interests
  2. TIPS FOR SOIL PREPRATION
    The soil that will be used for growing crops is prepared by ploughing, levelling, and manuring. The act of ploughing involves digging and loosening the soil, which aids in good soil aeration. In the levelling process, the earth is dispersed uniformly and levelled. The soil is then fertilised. Several factors, including soil texture, compaction, cation exchange capability, and organic matter content, might affect crop development. You have a better chance of farming success if you plant crops that are compatible with the properties of your soil. Growers can adjust soil characteristics by using certain cultivation techniques, adding amendments, utilising specific planting techniques, or using technologies to change variables like soil temperature, moisture retention, and other things.
  3. TIPS FOR PLANTING SEEDS
    The primary step in sowing is choosing seeds from high-quality crop strains. Sowing is the process of dispersing these seeds across a field once the soil has been prepared. Manual labour, hand labour, and seed drilling machinery are all acceptable methods of sowing seeds. In a small area, some crops, like paddy, are first raised as seedlings before being moved to the main field. The majority of farmers are unable to afford superior seeds because of their excessive costs.
